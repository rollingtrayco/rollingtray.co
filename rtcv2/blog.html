Keeping Your Tray in Prime Condition

A hardwood rolling tray is an investment in your ritual. With a few simple steps, you can ensure it remains a beautiful and functional centerpiece for years, even decades, to come. Wood is a natural material, and a little care goes a long way.

Regular Cleaning

After each session, simply use a soft, dry cloth or a soft-bristled brush (like a clean paintbrush) to wipe away any loose material. For more stubborn residue, a slightly damp cloth is all you need. Never submerge your tray in water or use harsh chemical cleaners, as this will damage the wood and the finish.

Conditioning the Wood (The Most Important Step)

Every month or two, or whenever the wood starts to look a little dry or dull, it's time to re-condition it. This nourishes the wood and protects it from drying out.

  1. Apply a small amount of food-safe mineral oil or a specialized wood butter/wax to a clean, soft cloth.
  2. Gently rub the conditioner into the wood, following the direction of the grain. Make sure to cover the entire surface, including the cutouts and edges.
  3. Let it sit and soak in for at least 20-30 minutes (or even overnight).
  4. With another clean cloth, buff off any excess. The wood will have a renewed, satin sheen.

What to Avoid

  • Direct Sunlight: Prolonged exposure to sunlight can fade the wood's natural colour.
  • Extreme Temperatures: Avoid leaving your tray near a radiator or in a cold car, as drastic temperature changes can cause the wood to warp or crack.
  • Dishwashers: Absolutely never put your tray in a dishwasher.

That's it! With minimal effort, your tray will not only perform flawlessly but will also develop a richer character and patina over time.
